§ 01

Who initiates, who decides, who is notified

Every workflow begins with one actor's intent or a system-detected event. Most disputes about "who owns this" disappear when the initiator is named.

Actors
Lead advisor

The lawyer-of-record

Usually a senior associate or partner. Owns the client relationship.
Initiates
New contracts, amendments, breach responses, counterparty negotiations.
Decides
Which clauses to flag, what advice to send to client, how to respond to counterparty.
Is notified
Covenant breaches, client questions, counterparty markups, payment exceptions.
Client treasurer

The decision maker

CFO, treasurer, or head of finance at the corporate. Signs and pays.
Initiates
Questions, amendment requests, payment confirmations, comments on advisor drafts.
Decides
Whether to sign, whether to fund, which option to pick on a breach.
Is notified
Payments due, obligations coming up, advisor recommendations, breach decisions.
Reviewing advisor

The second pair of eyes

Partner reviewing associate's drafts. Read-mostly role.
Initiates
Markups and comments on draft clauses. Approvals. Escalations.
Decides
Sign-off to send draft to client; sign-off to send to counterparty.
Is notified
Draft ready for review, urgent clauses flagged, approval requested.
Counterparty

The other side

Bank, supplier, or their counsel. May or may not be on Nomos.
Initiates
Term sheets, markups, payment notifications, amendment proposals.
Decides
Whether to accept proposed terms. Execution.
Is notified
Via Nomos link (if onboarded) or e-mail + PDF package (if not).

§ 03

Four core workflows, end-to-end

Read left → right. Each lane is an actor; each node is one surface in the product. Arrows are triggers (what makes the next step happen).

Swim lanes

A · New contract — advisor-initiated

From engagement letter to executed agreement. The most common origination path.

B · Payment cycle — system-initiated

Recurring obligation. The system computes, the client confirms. The advisor only sees it if something is off.

C · Covenant breach — system-detected

Oracle input changes. System alerts advisor first (triage), advisor frames decision for client.

D · Amendment request — client-initiated

Client asks, advisor drafts, counterparty signs. The only flow where the client is the starting actor.

§ 04

How people talk to each other

Every conversation is anchored to a contract and a clause. There is no general "inbox" — messages live where the work is.

 —
Principles
  1. One contract, one thread space. All messages about a contract live on that contract. The advisor's "Today" is a projection across contracts, not a parallel inbox.
  2. Comments anchor to clauses. A question about §4(a)(ii) lives on §4(a)(ii). Context is never lost.
  3. Client never talks to counterparty. Every external message is advisor-mediated, even if the advisor is just forwarding.
  4. Reviewing advisor is invisible to client. Internal review happens without the client ever seeing the back-and-forth.
  5. System speaks via advisor for legal matters, directly for operational ones. Breach detection → goes to advisor. Payment due → goes to client. Never reversed.
