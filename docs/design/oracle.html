Three layers of signal, one lens on your book.

§ 01

Design rationale

Four commitments that shape every Oracle surface. If a feature breaks one of them, it's on the wrong map.

Principles
01 · Impact first The Oracle never opens on "the news." It opens on "here are the 3 contracts in your book that something happened to today." Feeds live below the fold.
02 · One grammar Rates, events, regulation — all three layers resolve to the same sentence: signal → scope → which of your contracts → recommended action.
03 · Auto-diagrammed, not auto-summarised For regulation, Nomos doesn't write you a paragraph. It draws you a diagram: rule, scope test, your contracts caught, timeline.
04 · AI as a quiet tool "Ask the Oracle" lives as a single slim input at the top. It answers in cards anchored to contracts, not in a chat transcript. Useful, not central.
05 · Two surfaces, same object The front-page square and the full tab are the same data, different compression. The square is a horizon; the tab is the atlas.
06 · Quiet by default No ticker noise. The Oracle is closer to a weather report than a Bloomberg terminal. If nothing changed, it says so — and that is the useful answer.
§ 02

The square on Today

A 1:1 tile on the landing page. One line per layer — the single most consequential signal in each, today. Dark card, so it reads as an instrument panel against the paper page.

What the square does

Compresses the whole Oracle into three lines and a count. The rule is strict: one signal per layer, and only if it touches a contract in your book.

If nothing is happening

The square shows "quiet" — a single line like "No rate moves, no events, no regulatory activity touching your book since 09:00." This is a feature. The absence of alarm is information.

Interaction

  • Click any line → drops you into that layer in the full tab, scrolled to the signal.
  • Click the header → opens the full Oracle tab, impact-first view.
  • Hover → expands to show the next 2 signals per layer, in place.

Why dark

The rest of Today is warm paper — deliberately calm. The Oracle's one job is to be noticed when it has something to say. Contrast does the work.
